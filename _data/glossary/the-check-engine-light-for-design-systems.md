A car metaphor the course uses for a design system that appears to run fine on the surface while underlying issues go unaddressed. Most teams sense something is off but can't tell whether it's minor or serious, so the course maps damage to three tiers: a loose gas cap (cosmetic problems like inconsistent layer names, off token colors, and components that don't follow the rules), an alternator (mid-level decay such as detached instances, hardcoded values, and missing component properties that slowly erode the structure that makes the system useful), and a transmission (severe problems like no semantic structure, no metadata, and no connection to code, the kind of damage that makes AI tools generate garbage and cause more harm than good).
