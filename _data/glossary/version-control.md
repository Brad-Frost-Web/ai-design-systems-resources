Version control is a system that keeps track of files and the changes made to them over time, letting you step through the history of any file, undo things, roll back, and revert to previous versions. The course describes this snapshot-over-time as the heart and soul of what Git does, and stresses its value in the AI age as a safe way to explore and refine code without blowing up the live website or shipping unwanted changes to production.
