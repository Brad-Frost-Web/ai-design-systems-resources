Branching, which the course calls perhaps the most important concept in working with Git, creates a safe parallel timeline where you can do whatever you want — refactor, experiment, even burn the whole thing down — without touching the main branch, which is typically the live website or published library. Branches used for a specific piece of work are often called feature branches; you can create as many as you want to explore and add things while keeping main stable. Once a feature branch's work is committed and reviewed, it is ready to be merged into main, after which the branch can be safely deleted.
