An issue is a task that captures a unit of work — what you're exploring, the nature and priority of the work, and its dependencies — and gives people a place to have a back-and-forth conversation about it. Issues can be typed as enhancements or bugs, and can be closed once the work (such as a merged pull request) is resolved. The course stresses that issues matter especially in the AI age because they let more non-developers participate in the code process and capture rich context.
