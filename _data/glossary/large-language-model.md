A type of AI trained on massive amounts of text to understand and generate human language. The course explains that LLMs are trained on the open web (blogs, news, Stack Overflow, Reddit, images) and learn patterns in language rather than facts — essentially a predictive engine that repeatedly chooses the most likely next word, with billions of parameters encoding those patterns. They are good at writing, summarizing and translating text, answering questions and explaining concepts, generating and reviewing code, and reasoning through multi-step problems. The course's recurring metaphor: the LLM is the brain (it reasons and generates) and the engine of the stack — but an engine by itself doesn't drive anywhere.
